Snowboarding is a true art form. Whether it's jibbing off rails, speeding through massive jumps, or just buttering through waves of powder, every move is unique to the rider's style. It opens the doors to relaxation, and perserverance through hard work. It's extremely tough at first, but becomes much easier and fluid - essentially muscle memory. I can recall the first few times I road down a mountain which was the most frustrating time of my learning curve. Eventually everything becomes second nature and new tricks are easier to pick up than when you started. Everything about the sport is magnificently beautiful.
